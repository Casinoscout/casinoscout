How wagering requirements work — and why the maths matters

When a casino gives you a bonus, they attach a multiplier to it. That multiplier is the number of times you have to bet the bonus before you're allowed to withdraw. A 35x requirement on a C$100 bonus means C$3,500 in total bets placed before the money is yours to keep.

The part people miss: it's usually the bonus that gets multiplied, not the deposit. But some casinos (fewer these days) multiply both together, which doubles the work. The calculator defaults to "bonus only" because that's the more common structure — check your casino's actual terms to confirm.

Jackpot City — what 35x actually looks like in practice:
You deposit C$100 and receive a 100% match of C$100. The 35x applies to the bonus only. C$100 × 35 = C$3,500 in total bets before you can withdraw At C$2/spin on slots (roughly 600 spins an hour): about 2h 55min of play. Do-able within their 7-day expiry if you play regularly.
What changes when wagering applies to deposit + bonus: (C$100 deposit + C$100 bonus) × 35 = C$7,000 required Same bonus, twice the work. This is why reading "bonus only" vs "deposit + bonus" in the terms actually matters — it's not fine print, it's the difference between 3 hours and 6 hours of play.

Why playing blackjack on a slot bonus is almost always a mistake

Every casino applies a contribution rate to each game — the percentage of each bet that actually counts toward your wagering requirement. Slots are 100%, meaning every dollar you bet clears a dollar of requirement. Blackjack is typically 10%, sometimes less.

This trips up a lot of players who prefer table games. The maths is brutal:

Same C$14,000 requirement, two different games:
Blackjack at 10% contribution: C$14,000 ÷ 10% = C$140,000 in actual blackjack hands needed Slots at 100% contribution: C$14,000 ÷ 100% = C$14,000 in slot spins needed Here's the counterintuitive part: even though blackjack has a lower house edge (around 0.5% vs 4% for slots), the slot player is still better off. Clearing C$14,000 on slots costs roughly C$560 in expected losses. Clearing C$140,000 on blackjack costs roughly C$700. Slots win.

Win caps — the condition buried in the fine print

A win cap limits how much you can withdraw from bonus winnings, regardless of how much you actually win. Most casino reviewers don't mention it prominently. We think it's one of the most important numbers on the page.

Questions we get asked a lot

What is a wagering requirement?+
It's the number of times you have to bet your bonus before you can withdraw. Written as a multiplier — 35x means your total bets must equal 35 times the bonus amount. On a C$100 bonus that's C$3,500 in bets placed before the money becomes withdrawable. Also called a playthrough requirement.
What counts as a good wagering requirement in Canada?+
The Canadian market sits around 30–40x. Under 30x is genuinely player-friendly. Above 50x we'd say skip it — the house edge will eat the bonus value before you clear. Jackpot City and Spin Casino are both 35x on bonus only, which is below average. Jackbit has 0x on its free spins, which is unique in Canada.
Are there any real 0x wagering casinos in Canada?+
One in our verified top 6: Jackbit. Their welcome free spins have no playthrough attached — winnings up to C$100 are immediately withdrawable. There is a 1x deposit turnover before you can withdraw anything, which is standard. Every other major Canadian casino we track requires at least 35x wagering on welcome bonuses.
Do blackjack and roulette count toward wagering?+
They count, but at a reduced rate — usually 5–10% per bet instead of 100% for slots. So C$10 in blackjack bets only clears C$1 of wagering requirement. The practical result: playing table games on a bonus is almost always a worse deal than just playing slots, even though blackjack has a lower house edge. The contribution rate kills the advantage.
How do free spins wagering requirements actually work?+
The wagering applies to your winnings from the spins, not a fixed cash amount. If 100 spins at C$0.20 each produce C$18 in winnings and the requirement is 40x, you need to wager C$720 before withdrawing that C$18. The value per spin — usually C$0.10 to C$0.20 — is the number that most casinos bury in the terms. Switch to the Free Spins tab in this calculator and enter the actual per-spin value.
What is a win cap and does it affect every bonus?+
A win cap limits how much you can take out from bonus winnings. Not every bonus has one — Jackpot City and Spin Casino have no stated win cap on their deposit bonuses. But free spins bonuses almost always do. BitStarz caps no-deposit spins at C$150, 7Bit caps deposit free spin winnings at C$200, Jackbit caps at C$100. Is a casino bonus actually worth taking?+
Mathematically, most of them aren't — the expected clearing cost exceeds the bonus value. A C$400 bonus at 35x needs C$14,000 in bets. At 96% RTP that costs roughly C$560 in expected losses, making the net value about −C$160. But if you'd be playing those hours anyway, the bonus is essentially free money for time you'd already spend. Can I withdraw my deposit while a bonus is active?+
Usually yes — your deposit stays yours. But withdrawing cancels the bonus and you lose any winnings tied to it. At some casinos the deposit itself is locked until you complete wagering — check the specific terms before depositing if there's any chance you'll want to exit early. This varies significantly between casinos and bonus types.
